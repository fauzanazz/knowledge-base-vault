# Startup Technology Strategy

Startup technology strategy requires balancing rapid execution with sustainable technical foundations while operating under resource constraints.

## Core Principles

### Speed vs Quality Trade-offs
- **MVP Development**: Build minimum viable products for market validation
- **Technical Debt**: Accept short-term compromises for faster delivery
- **Iterative Improvement**: Refactor and improve based on user feedback
- **Premature Optimization**: Avoid over-engineering before product-market fit

### Technology Choices

#### Programming Languages and Frameworks
- **Team Expertise**: Leverage existing team knowledge
- **Hiring Pool**: Consider available talent in the market
- **Ecosystem Maturity**: Choose established technologies with good support
- **Scalability Path**: Ensure technology can grow with the business

#### Build vs Buy Decisions
- **Core Competency**: Build what differentiates your product
- **Commodity Services**: Buy standard functionality (auth, payments, email)
- **Time to Market**: Prioritize speed for non-differentiating features
- **Cost Analysis**: Consider total cost of ownership

## Scaling Considerations

### Team Growth
- **Hiring Strategy**: Plan for rapid team expansion
- **Onboarding Process**: Streamline new developer integration
- **Knowledge Transfer**: Document critical systems and decisions
- **Culture Establishment**: Define engineering values early

### Technical Scaling
- **[[System Scaling]]**: Plan for traffic growth patterns
- **Database Strategy**: Choose scalable data storage solutions
- **Infrastructure**: Cloud-first approach for flexibility
- **Monitoring**: Implement observability from day one

## Common Startup Mistakes

### Over-Engineering
- Building for scale before achieving product-market fit
- Implementing complex architectures prematurely
- Choosing bleeding-edge technologies without clear benefits

### Under-Engineering
- Ignoring security and compliance requirements
- Skipping automated testing and deployment
- Accumulating unsustainable technical debt

## Due Diligence Preparation

Startups should maintain:
- **Architecture Documentation**: System design and technology choices
- **Security Practices**: Data protection and access controls
- **Code Quality Metrics**: Test coverage and technical debt assessment
- **Scalability Plans**: Growth capacity and infrastructure roadmap

## Funding Stage Considerations

### Pre-Seed/Seed
- Focus on product validation
- Minimal viable architecture
- Small, versatile team

### Series A/B
- Establish scalable foundations
- Implement proper development processes
- Plan for team growth

### Growth Stage
- Optimize for performance and reliability
- Implement advanced monitoring and analytics
- Consider [[Microservices Architecture]] if appropriate

Successful startup technology strategy adapts to changing business needs while maintaining development velocity and team productivity.
