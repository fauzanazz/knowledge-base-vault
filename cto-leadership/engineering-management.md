# Engineering Management

Engineering management requires balancing technical expertise with people leadership skills to build high-performing development teams.

## The Engineer-Manager Pendulum

Many successful technologists alternate between individual contributor and management roles throughout their careers. This pendulum allows for:
- Maintaining technical relevance
- Gaining diverse perspectives
- Building empathy for both roles
- Career flexibility and growth

## Core Responsibilities

### People Management
- **1:1 Meetings**: Regular individual check-ins for career development and feedback
- **Performance Reviews**: Structured evaluation and goal setting
- **Career Development**: Mentoring and growth planning
- **Team Dynamics**: Fostering collaboration and resolving conflicts

### Technical Leadership
- **Architecture Decisions**: Guiding technical direction and standards
- **Code Quality**: Establishing review processes and best practices
- **Technical Debt**: Balancing feature delivery with system health
- **Technology Choices**: Evaluating tools and frameworks

### Process Optimization
- **Development Workflow**: Implementing effective [[Development Process]] practices
- **Project Planning**: Coordinating deliverables and timelines
- **Quality Assurance**: Ensuring testing and deployment standards
- **Metrics and Monitoring**: Tracking team performance and system health

## Maker's vs Manager's Schedule

Engineering managers must navigate between:
- **Maker Time**: Blocks for deep technical work
- **Manager Time**: Meetings, interruptions, and people issues

Effective managers protect their team's maker time while handling managerial responsibilities.

## Common Challenges

- **Technical Skill Decay**: Staying current while managing
- **Context Switching**: Balancing multiple priorities and interruptions
- **Difficult Conversations**: Performance issues and career guidance
- **Scaling Teams**: Growing from individual contributor to team leader

## Management Readmes

Many successful managers create "management readmes" documenting their:
- Communication preferences
- Meeting styles
- Feedback approaches
- Pet peeves and motivations

Engineering management success requires continuous learning and adaptation to team and organizational needs.
